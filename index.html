Chuck Jones sitting with the characters he's contributed to, with each drawn in his iconic style.

Timeline

  • 1912 - Jones is born in Spokane, Washington
  • 1932 - Jones graduated from the Choinard Art Institute and began working for Ubbe Iwerks starting as a cel washer
  • 1933 - Jones joined Leon Schlesinger Productions as an assistant animator and began working on Merrie Melodies and Looney Tunes
  • 1938 - Jones became a director at the production studio creating his first character Sniffles
  • 1957 - He directed the short What's Opera Doc
  • 1963 - Jones was terminated from Warner Bros. after moonlighting to work with United Productions of America on the film Gay Purr-ee
  • 1963 - Jones created his own animation studio Sib Tower 12 which was contracted by Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer to work on new Tom and Jerry cartoons and then acquired the studio in 1964
  • 1966 - Worked with Dr Seuss to help create the How the Grinch Stole Christmas animated short
  • 1970 - MGM closed their animation division and Jones created his own studio: Chuck Jones Enterprises
  • 1976 - Jones continues working with Warner Bros on the TV adaptation of The Carnival of the Animals
  • 1997 - Chuck Jones directs his final Looney Tunes cartoon From Hare to Eternity
  • 1997 - Jones is awarded the Edward MacDowell Medal
  • 1999 - Jones founds the non-profit Chuck Jones Center for Creativity
  • 2002 - Passes away at the age of 89
